Monthly Kansas City Women in Technology Scholarship

Kansas City Women in Technology is working hard to lower barriers for women getting into technology. We know cost can be a factor so we offer two (2) KCWiT scholarships each month for attendees who face income challenges.

Applications will be ranked by demonstration of the following criteria each month to determine selection*:

  • Financial need
  • Reason for attending
  • Commitment to ongoing learning
  • Willingness to help others with skills gained

GitHub Diversity Scholarship

Kansas City Women in Technology alongside GitHub is dedicated to helping women of all backgrounds have access to technology education in our programs. Thanks to GitHub’s sponsorship we are pleased to be able to offer three(3) scholarships per month to attendees of underrepresented demographics. Want to find out more about how GitHub is working on diversity & inclusion? Read more here.

Scholarships from the GitHub Diversity Scholarship are need-based, and are available for three(3) attendees each month. Women (cis and trans) who are part of an underrepresented demographic in technology (African American, Latinx, and Native American, including Alaskan and Hawaiian) are eligible to apply for scholarships from this fund. Scholarships are awarded based on financial need, the applicant's ability to meet requirements, and the applicant’s level of commitment to ongoing learning and paying it forward. These spots are guaranteed even when events are sold out.

Applications will be ranked by demonstration of the following criteria each month to determine selection:

  • Reason for attending
  • Commitment to ongoing learning
  • Willingness to help others with skills gained

*Those approved for financial aid scholarships that fail to comply with requirements will be marked as ineligible for any scholarship funds for a six-month period. Requirements include but are not limited to attending the session.
